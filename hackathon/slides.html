Quiz Buzzer Hackathon

Build a multiplayer quiz game with physical buzzers

3 hours. 3 buzzers. 1 winner.

The Idea

A pub quiz, but with real buzzer buttons, LED light shows, sound effects, and teams playing from their own devices.

  • Teams buzz in by smashing a physical button
  • Pick A, B, or C on your phone/laptop
  • The room reacts -- lights, sounds, drama
  • Scores, insults, and a dramatic finale

What We Have

Hardware

  • 3 USB buzzer buttons -- big, satisfying, loud
  • 1 USB RGB LED strip -- Google custom device
  • 1 Raspberry Pi -- on the network
  • A big HDMI display + meeting room with GVC

Software

  • Your corp laptop
  • Your language of choice
  • 3 hours of your time
  • Creativity

Real-World Constraints

  • USB HID policy -- buzzers present as keyboards. Not allowed on corp laptops. (Not technically blocked, just policy.)
    Solution: buzzers go into the RPi.
  • LEDs are fine -- they're Google custom devices, allowed to connect to corp machines.
  • No peer-to-peer on corp/guest WiFi -- devices can't talk to each other directly.
    Solution: we'll use a phone's WiFi hotspot for the game network.

Potential Tasks

RPi Buzzer Server

Read button presses, serve state over the network. Handle 3 identical devices.

Game Master

The brain -- game loop, scoring, coordinating buzzers + display + sound + LEDs.

Team Client

What teams see on their devices. Join, pick a name, answer questions.

Projector Display

What the audience sees. Questions, scores, drama. Make it look good.

Sound

Victory jingles, wrong-answer sad trombones, countdown tension. Procedural or sourced.

LED Reverse Engineering

Figure out the protocol. The device name is a clue -- find the internal docs.

Hardware IDs

  • Buzzers: 0x2341:0xC036 -- USB HID, presents as keyboard
  • LED strip: 0x18d1:0x5035 -- the product name will lead you to the protocol
  • RPi: SSH access, IP will be announced. user@<ip>

Contracts (suggested HTTP APIs) are in the handout. Use them, adapt them, or design your own.
